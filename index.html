Our story

Legend has it that the first Fijian god, Degei, passed down two very important crops to his four sons. The first was yuga, a type of hardwood and the second was kava, a euphoric and entheogenic drink brewed from roots of the yaqone plant. It is said that the four brothers used yuga to build the first settlements and drank kava to shape the peaceful and relaxed nature of Fijians today.

Together the brothers created paradise for the first people of Fiji. Joy and prosperity flooded through the new world.

In modern Fiji, Kava is drank to celebrate the four sons devotion to happiness by sitting around a fire, telling Degei's stories and raising a half coconut of kava to the good times. Some still say that kava grows wherever the descendants of the four sons reside.

So What is kava?

Traditionally drank in Fiji and other South Pacific Islands, we thought it was about time our friends in the west discovered our proudest secret.

Kava Bula is a non-alcoholic beverage that shares a lot of similarities to conventional alcohol, but without the nasty bits!

What's it made of?

Conventionally, kava is made from the roots of the yaqone plant. Firstly, we let the roots dry out, grind them into a powder and mix with water. The root contains kavalactones which is what makes it so similar to alcohol.

To make kava more yummy we've added some classic Fijian flavours, and also carbonated it which makes it extra refreshing on a hot summer's day.

What does Kava do?

Like alcohol, kava is a sedative, but also a euphoric and entheogenic, which in our eyes makes it the perfect socialising and entertaining beverage.

However, unlike alcohol, kava is not a toxin therefore does not have any negative health benefits and won't give you a nasty hangover in the morning. Now that's something we can all bula to!
